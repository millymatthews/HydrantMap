Cambridge Hydrant Locator


Go to Map >>>

The Cambridge Hydrant Locator is a prototype mapping application, designed for the residents of Cambridge to detect and display localised fire hydrants in emergency response situations (e.g. fire incidence) and for neighbourhood management (e.g. winter snow clearance and maintenance).

There are 19011 Cambridge fire hydrants currently maintained by the Engineering group of Cambridge Water Department2. The city boasts a Class 1 fire department due to the relatively high fire risk caused by a significant presence of old wooden, three story residential housing throughout the area.

In 2016 alone, there were 550 building fires attended to by the city fire department 3, including a devastating blaze that destroyed 16 buildings in one street. 4.


How it works


1) detects and positions a user on the map

2) calculates a 200-metre buffer around the user location

3) displays all fire hydrants within the buffer


To use the 'my location' button on the map, a user must share or turn on their device's location. If a devices location is not shared, panning to an area of interest in the city will trigger the map to instead recalculate the buffer based on the centre of the map. Hydrants are colour categorised by flow rate, and can be individually selected to show their rate in Gallons Per Minute (GPM). Hydrants will not be drawn outside of the limits of the city.


Mapping Software


This web map is generated using the LeafletJS web mapping library, and all rendering of data and visualization of points is performed by LeafletJS on the client device. Basemap tiles are sourced from Mapbox, using OpenStreetMap data. All advanced geospatial analysis within the map are computed using functions from the Turf.JS library.
